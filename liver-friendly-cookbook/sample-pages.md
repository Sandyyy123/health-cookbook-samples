## RECIPE #1 — TURMERIC LEMON BAKED SALMON
**Category: Hearty Dinners | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 20 min | Serves: 2 | Difficulty: Easy**

---

### WHY IT WORKS

Salmon is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which help reduce liver inflammation and support healthy fat processing. Turmeric contains curcumin — one of the most studied natural compounds for liver cell protection.

---

### INGREDIENTS

- 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley to garnish (optional)

---

### INSTRUCTIONS

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Place salmon fillets skin-side down on the prepared baking sheet.
3. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, turmeric, garlic powder, and lemon juice.
4. Spoon the mixture evenly over both fillets. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
5. Bake for 18-20 minutes, until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately with steamed vegetables or brown rice.

---

### NUTRITION NOTE

This dish is naturally low in sodium and free from processed ingredients. Omega-3s from salmon have been shown to reduce liver fat accumulation — a key goal for anyone managing liver health. The lemon juice also supports bile production, which helps your liver process fats more efficiently.

---

### EASY SWAP

- No salmon? Use cod or tilapia — both are mild, affordable white fish with similar benefits.
- Dairy-free already, but if you want extra richness, add a thin slice of avocado on top before serving.
- Watching sodium closely? Skip the salt entirely — the lemon and turmeric provide plenty of flavor.

## RECIPE #2 — OVERNIGHT OATS WITH BERRIES AND FLAXSEED
**Category: Breakfasts | Prep: 5 min | Chill: Overnight | Serves: 1 | Difficulty: Very Easy**

---

### WHY IT WORKS

Oats provide soluble fiber that helps regulate blood sugar and reduce cholesterol — both important when supporting liver function. Flaxseed adds plant-based omega-3s and lignans, compounds that support hormone balance and reduce oxidative stress in liver cells.

---

### INGREDIENTS

- 1/2 cup rolled oats (not instant)
- 3/4 cup unsweetened almond milk (or low-fat dairy milk)
- 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed
- 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup
- 1/2 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen — blueberries, raspberries, or strawberries)
- A pinch of cinnamon

---

### INSTRUCTIONS

1. Add oats, almond milk, ground flaxseed, sweetener, and cinnamon to a mason jar or container with a lid.
2. Stir well until combined.
3. Seal and refrigerate overnight, or for at least 6 hours.
4. In the morning, give it a stir. If it's too thick, add a splash of milk.
5. Top with fresh or thawed berries and serve cold.

---

### NUTRITION NOTE

Berries — especially blueberries — are among the most liver-protective foods available. Their antioxidants help prevent oxidative damage to liver cells. This breakfast also has no refined sugar and a low glycemic load, which means it won't cause the blood sugar spikes that put extra strain on the liver.

---

### EASY SWAP

- No almond milk? Regular low-fat milk or oat milk work equally well.
- Ground flaxseed not available? Chia seeds are a direct substitute — same ratio.
- Want more protein? Stir in 2 tablespoons of plain Greek yogurt before refrigerating.

## RECIPE #3 — GARLIC AND HERB WHITE BEAN SOUP
**Category: Light Lunches | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 25 min | Serves: 4 | Difficulty: Easy**

---

### WHY IT WORKS

White beans are an excellent plant-based protein source that's low in fat and high in fiber — ideal for liver health because they support steady digestion without overloading the liver's processing workload. Garlic contains allicin, a natural compound that activates liver enzymes and supports the organ's natural detox processes.

---

### INGREDIENTS

- 2 cans (15 oz each) white beans (cannellini or great northern), drained and rinsed
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 stalks celery, sliced
- 2 medium carrots, sliced
- 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh lemon juice to finish

---

### INSTRUCTIONS

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until soft.
2. Add garlic, celery, and carrots. Cook for another 3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
3. Pour in the vegetable broth and add the drained beans, thyme, and rosemary.
4. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes until vegetables are tender.
5. Use the back of a spoon to mash a few beans against the side of the pot — this thickens the soup naturally without adding cream or flour.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning. Finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice before serving.

---

### NUTRITION NOTE

Using low-sodium broth keeps this soup gentle on the liver. Canned beans are perfectly healthy when rinsed — rinsing removes most of the added sodium from the canning liquid. This soup freezes well, making it an excellent meal-prep option for the week.

---

### EASY SWAP

- No white beans? Chickpeas work well and have a slightly nuttier flavor.
- Want more substance? Add a handful of baby spinach in the last 2 minutes of cooking.
- No fresh garlic? 1 teaspoon of garlic powder works, but fresh garlic provides the most benefit.
